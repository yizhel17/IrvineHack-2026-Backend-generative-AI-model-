Under the provisions of the California Revenue and Taxation Code, specifically Sections 3691 and 3692, real property that remains tax-defaulted for five (5) or more years is subject to the County Tax Collector's power to sell. Your property entered tax-defaulted status on July 1, 2015, which means it has now exceeded the statutory five-year redemption period and is eligible for sale at public auction.

IMPORTANT NOTICE REGARDING FORECLOSURE AND SALE

Your property is now eligible for tax sale. The Los Angeles County Treasurer and Tax Collector intends to sell the above-referenced tax-defaulted property at public auction unless the TOTAL AMOUNT REQUIRED TO REDEEM, including all accumulated penalties and interest, is paid in full prior to the date of sale. Upon sale, all rights, title, and interest in the property will be conveyed to the successful bidder, and all prior liens, mortgages, and encumbrances (except specific governmental liens as defined by law) will be extinguished. This process constitutes a forced sale of the property to recover the outstanding tax liabilities.

Personal checks are not accepted for redemption amounts due to processing time.
